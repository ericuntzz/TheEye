ANATOMY
Every decision has a reason
THIN LEFT / THICK RIGHT
Calligraphic high-contrast. The thin stroke is the upstroke of a broad-nib pen returning to the apex. The thick stroke is the downstroke under full pressure. The asymmetry is what makes the mark feel drawn, not constructed.
ENTASIS — BOTH FACES CURVE
The inner face bows 13.5px left. The outer face bows 8px left. Both faces of the right leg curve inward — like a column under load. The inner curve is deeper, preserving the calligraphic asymmetry.
SOLID WEDGE APEX
The outer silhouette peaks at a single point. The counter opens 16px below it. The wedge of solid material joins both legs physically — one unified form from tip to foot.
BRACKET SERIFS
Four tangent-matched concave curves at the serif joins. Each computed from the exact incoming angle of its leg. The serifs ground the mark at the base — it stands on the page rather than floating.
